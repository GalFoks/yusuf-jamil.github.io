Customer Churn Prediction — End-to-End ML Pipeline

JUN 2026
PythonXGBoostSMOTE SHAPLIMEFastAPIStreamlit

Problem

A telecom provider had no systematic way to flag at-risk customers, and a default 50% cutoff ignored that missing a churner costs roughly 3x more than a false alarm (Rp 500K vs. Rp 150K).

Approach

EDA and 8 engineered features (incl. a contract risk score and a high-charge-new-customer flag) on 7,043 Telco customers, SMOTE-balanced XGBoost tuned via GridSearchCV, and a decision threshold re-optimized to 0.32 against real cost trade-offs.

Result

88% recall on churners at ≈ Rp 80.6M total operational cost; SHAP/LIME pointed to contract type and tenure as the dominant drivers, shipped via a FastAPI endpoint and a Streamlit dashboard.

Causal Impact of Platform Discounts on Seller Margin

JUN 2026
PythonSQL · MySQLOLS IV/2SLSDiDstatsmodels

Problem

Does funding seller discounts actually erode the margin sellers keep — or is the platform avoiding a relationship that doesn't exist?

Approach

A layered causal-inference pipeline — OLS with clustered standard errors, a Hausman endogeneity test, IV/2SLS with two instruments, and DiD with a placebo check — on 1,873 active sellers across 112,101 transactions from Olist's Brazilian e-commerce data, plus a transparently documented synthetic discount layer.

Result

No statistically significant evidence that discount intensity erodes net margin (2SLS β=+0.0021, p=0.575; DiD β=+0.053, p=0.537). Placebo test passed (p=0.74), confirming the design held up — delivered with a constrained-optimization pricing model and a 26-page report.

1,873 Sellers · No Causal Effect Found github.com/GalFoks/marketplace-discount-seller-margin-causal ↗
